Who We Are

A Young Organization Built on a Simple Belief

We believe the people most affected by poverty, climate change, and inequality already hold the solutions. Our job is to provide resources, skills, and space for those solutions to grow.

ClimateWise was founded in 2021 by young Malawians who had seen, first-hand, what happens when outside organizations arrive with predetermined answers. We chose a different path — sitting with communities, asking what they need, and building programs that communities themselves sustain long after our project timelines end.

What Drives Us

Our Mission, Vision & Values

These aren't words on a wall. They are the decisions we make in the field every single day.

🎯 Our Mission

What We Do Every Day

To empower communities in Malawi with practical, community-led solutions in clean water, food security, climate resilience, and gender-inclusive education — so that every family can live with dignity, safety, and opportunity.

We don't arrive with the answer. We arrive with resources, skills, and the discipline to listen first.

🌍 Our Vision

The Future We Are Building

A Malawi where every community has clean water within reach, food on the table, forests on the hills, and girls in the classroom — not because an organisation is present, but because communities have the knowledge, ownership, and confidence to sustain it themselves.

"We will know we have succeeded when communities no longer need us."

⚖️ Our Values

How We Show Up

Six principles guide every decision we make — from how we design a program to how we report results:

  • Community First — communities lead, we support
  • Honesty — we only claim what we can prove
  • Gender Intentionality — women centred in every program
  • Sustainability — programs must outlast our involvement
  • Youth Leadership — young people drive the work
  • Accountability — every donor kwacha is reported
Our Journey

From an Idea to 89 Communities

ClimateWise started with a conversation between young Malawians who were tired of watching their communities suffer problems that had workable solutions. Here is how we got from that conversation to where we are today.

2021 — Founded

ClimateWise Is Born

A group of young Malawians registers ClimateWise as a non-profit organisation. The founding focus: climate change is not an abstract global problem — it is the reason the borehole dried up, the harvest failed, and the girl didn't come back to school. We commit to community-led solutions in four areas.

Registered Non-Profit Youth-Led 4 Focus Areas Set
2022 — First Projects

First Boreholes, First Farmers Trained

We drill our first boreholes in Chikwawa District, bringing clean water to over 4,000 people in T/A Kasisi. Simultaneously, we launch our first Farmers Field Schools in Dowa District, training farmers in conservation agriculture. Cholera cases begin to fall. Yields begin to rise.

First Boreholes Drilled Farmers Field Schools Launched 4,000+ People Reached
2023 — UNESCO Partnership

Gender Equality Program Launched with UNESCO

We receive funding from UNESCO to launch a gender equality and education program across the Central Region. Girls' school attendance begins climbing from 40%. We establish our first gender clubs. The Agroforestry Restoration Initiative (ARI) also begins — training 39 community leaders and establishing four tree nurseries.

UNESCO Partnership ARI Launched 100 Gender Clubs Formed
2024 — Scale & Depth

Growing Reach Without Losing Quality

We scale carefully — expanding WASH projects to Ntcheu District, adding beekeeping livelihoods to the ARI program, and launching youth entrepreneurship training that gives birth to businesses like Tinashe and Maggie's mushroom farm. We reach 89 communities and plant 10,110 trees. Girls' attendance hits 85%.

89 Communities Youth Entrepreneurship 10,110 Trees Planted
2025–Present — Where We Are Now

23,651 People. Still Growing Responsibly.

Today ClimateWise serves 23,651 people with clean water, 2,470 farmers with training and market access, 5,154 students through gender programs, and 89 communities with climate preparedness plans. We are actively seeking partnerships to expand our WASH and agriculture work while maintaining the community-first quality that makes results last.

Our Approach

Six Principles That Make Our Work Last

Most community programs fail within two years of the organisation leaving. Ours don't. Here is why.

Principle 01 👂

Listen Before You Plan

Every program begins with community consultations — not surveys from an office. We sit with women, men, youth, and elders to understand the real problem before we design any solution.

Principle 02 🤝

Communities Own the Results

We train Water Point Committees, Village Forest Committees, and Farmer Groups to manage every asset we create. The day we leave, communities are already running things themselves.

Principle 03 ♀

Women at the Centre

65% of our agriculture beneficiaries are women. All our WASH designs centre women's safety. Our gender program exists because equal opportunity is not optional — it is essential.

Principle 04 📊

We Measure What Matters

Every project has baseline data, midline checks, and endline reporting. We measure cholera cases, school attendance, crop yields, and tree survival — because impact should be provable, not just claimed.

Principle 05 🌱

Build to Last, Not to Impress

We drill boreholes with local maintenance capacity. We plant trees with native species suited to local soil. We train farmers in techniques that don't require ongoing external input. Sustainability is not a word — it is a design requirement.

Principle 06 🔍

Radical Transparency

Every number on our website comes from a field report. We publish impact data. We send donors updates. We never claim a partnership we don't have. If you want to verify a figure, ask us — we will send you the evidence.

For Funders & Partners

What You Need to Know Before You Decide

We understand that funders have many choices and limited resources. Here is everything a serious funder needs to know about ClimateWise — in plain language, with nothing hidden.

Published Impact Reports
Every project has a published field report. Donors can read exactly what happened, who was reached, and what changed — before and after.
Verified Beneficiary Counts
We count beneficiaries through community registers, school attendance records, and Water Point Committee data — not estimates. 23,651 is a real number.
Baseline & Endline Measurement
We measure before we start and after we finish. Girls' attendance was 40% before the program. It is 85% now. We can show you both data points.
Financial Accountability
We are a registered non-profit (Reg No: MW/CW/0225) with clear financial records. We report expenditure to all funders. Every kwacha is accounted for.
Credible Institutional Partners
Our programs are co-funded and validated by UNESCO, USAID, and the Government of Malawi — partners who conduct their own due diligence before committing.
