Who We Are

Rooted in Malawi, driven by community ownership.

ClimateWise Organization is a youth-led nonprofit founded in 2021 and headquartered in Lilongwe, Malawi. We empower vulnerable communities to build resilience against climate change, poverty, and inequality through integrated, community-owned solutions.

Our work spans Water, Sanitation & Hygiene (WASH), Sustainable Agriculture, Gender Equality, and Environmental Conservation — directly impacting tens of thousands across Central, Southern, and Northern Malawi.

3 Regions, 10+ Districts

Lilongwe, Dowa, Ntchisi, Zomba, Phalombe, Chikwawa, Machinga, Chitipa, Rumphi, Karonga

Youth-Led Governance

🎯 Our Mission

Serve individuals and families in Malawi's most vulnerable communities.

We deliver integrated solutions in WASH, agriculture, gender equality, and environmental conservation — building lasting resilience from the ground up.

✨ Our Vision

A Malawi where every community thrives in harmony with nature.

"Every child learns in a good school, every girl grows up with the same chances as a boy, every family has enough to eat, access to quality water, and every community lives in harmony with the environment."

Our Strategic Pillars

Four interconnected programs, one vision for resilience.

Every pillar is designed to reinforce the others — because clean water enables farming, farming supports nutrition, and gender equality unlocks it all.

💧

Water, Sanitation & Hygiene (WASH)

11 boreholes constructed, 23,651+ people with sustainable water access, up to 90% reduction in cholera cases. Includes borehole rehabilitation, solar water systems, and Water Point Committees.

✓ 11 boreholes✓ 23,651+ people
🌾

Sustainable Agriculture & Food Security

532 farmers trained, yield increases up to 3x per acre (10→30 bags of maize), 28 youth-led agribusinesses created. Farmer field schools and market linkages.

✓ 532 farmers✓ 28 agribusinesses
⚖️

Gender Equality & Social Inclusion

5,154+ students reached, 100 Gender Clubs established in schools, GBV prevention workshops, leadership training for girls and young women.

✓ 100 clubs✓ 5,154+ students
🌳

Environment & Climate Resilience

10,110 trees planted, youth-led climate action across 6 districts, climate education integrated into community planning, renewable energy promotion.

✓ 10,110 trees✓ 6 districts
