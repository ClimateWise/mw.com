The Challenge

Climate change is rewriting the rules of farming.

Malawi's smallholder farmers — who produce 80% of the country's food — are on the front lines of climate change. Erratic rains, prolonged dry spells, and degraded soil mean harvests are shrinking while populations grow.

80%

of Malawi's food comes from smallholder farmers — yet most live below the poverty line.

Climate-Smart Innovation

Trees inside a groundnuts farm?

It looks strange at first. But there's genius in it.

The trees are nitrogen-fixers. They pull nutrients from deep underground and bring them to the surface. When their leaves fall, they become natural fertilizer for the groundnuts. The trees also provide shade, reduce water evaporation, and protect crops from wind.

One field. Two harvests. Groundnuts today. Trees for years. That's agroforestry. That's climate-smart.

🥜 Groundnuts = food in 3 months 🌳 Trees = food, timber, shade in 10 years
