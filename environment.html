The Crisis

Malawi is losing its green heart.

Every year, Malawi loses thousands of hectares of forest to charcoal production, tobacco curing, and agricultural expansion. The consequences are devastating: soil erosion, dried-up rivers, failed crops, and a warming climate.

33,000+

hectares of forest lost every year in Malawi — one of the highest deforestation rates in Africa.

2.6°C

temperature increase projected by 2050 if we don't act now. Our children will inherit a much hotter Malawi.

Malawi's Largest & Oldest Reserve

Restoring Nkhotakota Reserve.

Malawi's hidden wilderness is coming back to life.

Nkhotakota Wildlife Reserve is Malawi's largest and oldest reserve — 1,800 square kilometers of ancient miombo woodland, rushing rivers, and hidden waterfalls. But decades of poaching and deforestation left it silent.

Today, that story is changing. ClimateWise is proud to support restoration efforts in Nkhotakota — planting native trees, training communities in sustainable land use, and protecting one of Malawi's most precious ecosystems. Elephants are returning. Forests are regrowing. Hope is being restored.

Our Approach

Youth-led climate action from the ground up.

We don't just plant trees. We build a culture of environmental stewardship that lasts for generations.

Tree Planting & Nurseries

10,110 trees planted across 6 districts. Native species. Community-managed nurseries. Survival rates above 80%.

Environmental Education

Climate education integrated into school curricula and community planning. Children learn to love the land before they learn to use it.

Renewable Energy & Alternatives

Promoting clean cooking alternatives to charcoal. Reducing pressure on forests while improving household health.
