About ClimateWise

Transforming Malawi, One Community at a Time

ClimateWise Organization is a youth-led non-profit founded in 2021 and registered in Malawi. We work side-by-side with vulnerable communities to solve real problems caused by climate change, poverty, inequality, and lack of basic services.

We create a sustainable future by addressing climate change through innovative solutions, community-driven projects, and strategic partnerships. Join us to protect biodiversity, empower communities, and build climate resilience.

Registered nonprofit, accountable and transparent
Partnered with UNESCO, USAID, UNICEF and the Government of Malawi
100% of donations go directly to community programs
Youth-led with deep community roots since 2021
Learn More About Us →

Our mission is to empower communities through education, sustainable development practices aligned with the SDGs, and climate-resilient technologies — creating a future that is environmentally and economically sustainable for all Malawians.

Community-first approach in all program design
SDG-aligned interventions across all four focus areas
Evidence-based programming with measurable outcomes

Climate-Smart Agriculture
Chitipa DistrictFarmer Field School

From Hunger to Harvest in Northern Malawi

In Chitipa, one of the most remote districts in northern Malawi, farmers have suffered from unpredictable rainfall and declining soil fertility for years. Many families survived on one meal a day. Through our Farmer Field School program, we trained one hundred and twenty smallholder farmers in conservation agriculture techniques including minimum tillage, mulching, intercropping, and natural pest management. The results exceeded everyone's expectations.

Maize Yield Before8 bags/acre
Maize Yield After35 bags/acre

Clean Water & Sanitation
Chikwawa DistrictUSAID Funded

Ending the 4km Water Journey

Before our intervention, women and girls in T/A Kasisi walked four kilometers every day to collect contaminated water from the Shire River. They faced harassment and violence along the way. Cholera outbreaks were devastating these villages. After we installed a solar-powered borehole and trained a Water Point Committee with sixty percent women, the transformation was immediate.
