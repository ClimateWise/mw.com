The Reality

Dirty water steals more than health. It steals futures.

In rural Malawi, 6.8 million people still lack access to clean water. Women and girls walk an average of 4 kilometers every day to collect water — time they could spend in school, running a business, or resting.

3-4 hours

saved every day for women and girls when a borehole is built near their home.

1,000+

children under 5 die every year in Malawi from diarrheal diseases caused by unsafe water.

Our Solution

Simple technology. Community ownership. Lasting impact.

We don't just drill boreholes. We build systems that communities run themselves.

11 Boreholes Constructed

Across Ntchisi, Chikwawa, Kasungu, Machinga, and Mulanje districts. Each borehole serves 1,500-2,500 people.

Water Point Committees

Elected by the community, with over 60% women. Trained in maintenance, finance, and hygiene promotion.

Hygiene Education

Handwashing with soap, safe water storage, latrine use. Behavior change that saves lives.

Real Stories, Real Change

Meet the people behind the water.

These are not case studies. These are our neighbors, our partners, our heroes.

"No mother should bury her daughter because of water. I can't bring my sister back. But I can save someone else's sister."
— Esnat
Hygiene Trainer, Chikwawa

Esnat lost her sister to cholera. Now she has trained 47 households in her village on handwashing, safe water storage, and latrine use.

"I can fix a broken pump. I collect maintenance fees. I protect the borehole like it's my own. Because it IS mine."
— Chifundo
Water Committee Member

Chifundo is one of the women leading community-owned water systems. This is community ownership. This is how water lasts.

"A stick. A string. A bottle. A soap. Clean water. No electricity. No plumbing. No expensive parts. Just clean hands. Just saved lives."
— Mponda Magiya
Simple Handwashing Device

A simple handwashing device made from local materials. Simple technology. Big impact. That's ClimateWise.
